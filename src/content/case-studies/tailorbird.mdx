        <p class="cs-large-text">
          Amazon Robotics faces significant challenges in planning and implementing robotic systems across its global network of fulfillment centers. The company's operations span hundreds of facilities worldwide, requiring precise coordination of equipment deployments, upgrades, and maintenance schedules.
        </p>
        
        <p class="text-intro">
          The current process for creating and managing Plans of Record (PORs) for these installations is fragmented across organizations, with each using different methods. This manual and tedious approach leads to delays, errors, and communication gaps between teams. This fragmentation creates significant inefficiencies that impact the entire supply chain and deployment timeline.
        </p>
        
        <p>This project initiative came about due to the deprecation of an existing subscription to Clarity PPM, a commercial-off-the-shelf-software intended for project management use-cases. At the time of this project, Amazon Robotics' POR was being managed through Clarity PPM as the company had an existing subscription which was being used by World Wide Technology Deployment and Field Support (WWTD), an AR org largely responsible for managing on-the-ground activity within fulfillment centers. As of June 2025, Clarity PPM is to be deprecated as WWTD will have switched software solutions for their specific use cases and needs. With the subscription to Clarity PPM ending, this gap created a need for a new POR management solution.</p>
        
        <h3 class="cs-subtitle">What is POR?</h3>

It serves as the centralized system that tracks all changes, milestones, and requirements for national and international fulfillment centers. POR essentially lays the foundation for determining how much equipment and resources are needed to establish and maintain both new and existing sites.</p>

  <div class="container">
    <div class="cs-section-grid">
      <div class="cs-sidebar">
        <h2 class="cs-sidebar-title" data-section-id="reflections">Reflections</h2>
      </div>
      <div class="cs-content">
        <h3 class="cs-subtitle">Design Challenges</h3>
        
        <div class="reflection-grid">
          <div class="reflection-card">
            <div class="reflection-number">01</div>
            <h4 class="reflection-title">Accelerated Design Timeline</h4>
            <p class="reflection-description">
              The two-week design sprint for creating testable mockups required rapid decision-making and prioritization, forcing me to focus on high-impact UX areas first while ensuring adequate feature coverage.
            </p>
          </div>
          
          <div class="reflection-card">
            <div class="reflection-number">02</div>
            <h4 class="reflection-title">Real-time Technical Iteration</h4>
            <p class="reflection-description">
              Due to the compressed timeline, design and development overlapped significantly. I had to closely monitor and iterate with engineers on the fly as we encountered technical obstacles, particularly around database integration and validation logic. POR data is highly sensitive, and enabling automatic validation proved to be a substantial technical challenge that required constant designer-developer collaboration.
            </p>
          </div>
          
          <div class="reflection-card">
            <div class="reflection-number">03</div>
            <h4 class="reflection-title">Balancing Familiarity with Innovation</h4>
            <p class="reflection-description">
              Finding the right balance between maintaining familiar patterns from Clarity PPM while introducing meaningful UX improvements required careful consideration of user comfort versus efficiency gains.
            </p>
          </div>
          
          <div class="reflection-card">
            <div class="reflection-number">04</div>
            <h4 class="reflection-title">Competing Stakeholder Priorities</h4>
            <p class="reflection-description">
              Different stakeholder groups had competing visions for the interface, requiring diplomatic navigation and data-driven decision making to achieve consensus.
            </p>
          </div>
          
          <div class="reflection-card">
            <div class="reflection-number">05</div>
            <h4 class="reflection-title">Design System Constraints</h4>
            <p class="reflection-description">
              Working within the Cloudscape design system provided efficiency benefits but required creative adaptation for specialized POR workflows that didn't fit standard patterns.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>
        
        <h3 class="cs-subtitle">Key Learnings</h3>
        
        <div class="reflection-grid">
          <div class="reflection-card">
            <div class="reflection-number">01</div>
            <h4 class="reflection-title">Data vs. Perception Disconnect</h4>
            <p class="reflection-description">
              Users reported higher confidence with interfaces that objectively caused more errors (the confidence paradox), highlighting the critical importance of measuring both performance metrics and user perception.
            </p>
          </div>
          
          <div class="reflection-card">
            <div class="reflection-number">02</div>
            <h4 class="reflection-title">A/B Testing Value</h4>
            <p class="reflection-description">
              The focused A/B testing approach provided objective data that aligned stakeholders around user performance results rather than personal preferences, proving invaluable for decision-making under time pressure.
            </p>
          </div>
          
          <div class="reflection-card">
            <div class="reflection-number">03</div>
            <h4 class="reflection-title">Users Embrace Change When It Solves Pain Points</h4>
            <p class="reflection-description">
              Despite initial concerns about change management, users readily accepted significant interface changes when they directly addressed their workflow frustrations.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
